PROJECT PROPOSAL



[1.] Binti Shujaa.(Ndoto Yangu,Haitakufa)

‘’ Shujaa Project’’ focuses on addressing three key strategic areas, first school re-entry program for adolescence mothers, Capacity building program for out of schools adolescents female and Resilience of female adolescents towards teenage pregnancy. Project designed in a strategic and intergrated way towards finding a sustainable solutions for female adolescents. Teenage pregnancy exposes adolescent women to the serious threats in social, economic and medical aspect. They are in high risks o f dying in child birth, risk of living in poverty as single mothers, risks of death during illegal abortion and social exclusion as well. This program will cover three strategic dimensions above to create a synergic impacts.




[2.] ‘’Nipe Nafasi Niseme’’ (Ujasiri wangu Silaha yangu)

Nipe nafasi niseme-is a national campaign which has a mission of rescuing more children and breaking the silent of victims of abuse, The campaign is constructed to capture and addresses agendas of children, which have been neglected with some parents and community members as well. This is a 3 years’ campaign which aim to create more awareness to children and the whole communities to report all forms of abuses in domestics, at schools, either child-child or adult-child etc. recently domestic abuse is highly occurring in the society and is often not reported, all cases are informal closed, as family member’s agreeing themselves. Sometimes the abuse continues and the children’s remains with the pain. Many studies shows that, children who have experienced abuse and violance of different forms, they are not confidence, abuse and violance erodes the strong natural foundation that help children in leading a healthy safepath from childhood. This can be notified as many children express fear in introducing themselves, appearing infront of new people, high privacy, trusting few people or no anyone in their inner trust cycle, also they are not confident in presentation and last but not least fear to break the silent while there is risks of abuse. Getting to know, courage is a weapon for children for sustainable growth. Courage determines how children and young people can handle challenges, how they can best relate to others and make informed choices, to develop in a healthy way. Helps also to builds strong relationships and adapt to changes in dealing with life challenges. Courage can be eroded to children’s life through many ways including a continuation of abusive actions to children, lack parental skills which demonstrating positive parenting etc..



[3.] ‘’TUWALEE NYUMBANI

Tuwalee Nyumbani is a new project aim to provide a contemporary contribution to the implementation of parenting programs which enhancing quality care and mostly alternative care in Tanzania. Its the fact that the government of Tanzania is in the process of developing national guidelines on establishing and managing the alternative care systems in Tanzania. Project Goal aiming to see every child have an access of basic services and grow in natural environment of families where all rights are well preserved and protected. Outcome areas will be framed to foreseen access and availability of basic requirement for children, find support and link children to families for appropriate parental care. Also joining the coalition of other potential partners for strengthning advocacy role. Again to ensure it contribute towards quality alternative care for children in need both from the streets and from the wrong placement, program will work and investing in social protections programs that wherever possible prevent unnecessary shift of children to alternative care and children leaving home coming into streets. This is based on the understanding that children are usually better cared in their own home, with kin within their communities. Preventive measures are driven by the necessity principle, which aim to ensure that children only enter into alternative care when such provision is necessary.



[4.] Menstrual Health and Hygiene Practices (MHHP)

Menstrual Health and Hygiene Practices (MHHP) has been attracting a worldwide attention as it is estimated that menstrual is experienced by about one quarter of the global population. For this vital fact, a good practices of MHH ensures girls and women lives in friendly and supportive environment that values their dignity. Regardless of the attention that has been drawn about the matter, but the practice still remains poor for different factors in its complexity due to the nature of reasons and solutions required. Evidences drawn from different researches and other studies confirm that millions of adolescent girls worldwide denied the right to manage their MHH in a dignified way which consider their safety and healthy. Also it remains a low priority in low income countries due to limited resources and other socio-cultural factors, myths, belief and taboos. As there is a growing number of adolescents every year, girls will continue to be in a disadvantaged position due to under preparedness factor of low income countries to meet the demands for safe MHH. It is also the fact that girls with disabilites endure more challenges related to menstruation as compared to others without disabilities. Inclusiveness of special groups must be given a serious attention when preparing different programs.



[5.] Social Accountability Monitoring (SAM).

Social Accountability in the above context refer the broad range of actions and mechanisms that citizens can engage in to hold the state (represented by public officials and service providers) to account. (World Bank 2006). Evidences suggests that social accountability has greatest potential to effect positive improvements in the delivery of essential services. Are increasingly used to improve the perfomance of public service providers. Social accountability is effective tool against corruption. Corruption is the major hindrance factor of development in public service delivery. SAM is one that can empower citizens and promote responsive behaviours from public officials on demanding transparency, accountability and ultimate have a clear impact on improving people’s lives.



[6.] Nilinde Nijivunie Utamaduni Wangu.

Maasai culture is the most popular and one of the strongest tribe in Tanzania, which has managed to maintain its origin culture and transpacy it to generations. Many good practices has introduced the Nation inside and outside the boundary. Also is one among many Tanzanian Tribes where girls and womens rights are highly denied and disvalued dispite the fact that young ladies and women play a vital role to sustain the culture. There is a practice of FGM to the high extents, rights to education is denied for young girls, as they gives priority to early marriage adolescent girls. There is a strong cultural bonds which ties girls and women in a position of not able to exercise their full given rights. Many researches and studies has proved the consequences of bad cultural practices like FGM, Early marriage, denial of education rights etc.
